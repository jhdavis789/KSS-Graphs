Covenant Mechanics: Before vs After 2030

Event What Changes Why It Matters
TODAY 11 DCs pledged as collateral for 10% secured notes. ABL facility restricts asset dispositions, liens, and restricted payments. Negative pledge covenant prevents additional collateral pledging. Cannot execute large SLB — DCs are encumbered, ABL covenants restrict asset sales, lien restrictions prevent new secured financing on stores.
Jun 1, 2030
10% notes mature		 11 DCs released from security interest. $36M/yr interest expense eliminated. Lien restrictions on DCs lifted. Asset disposition covenant baskets expand with reduced leverage. SLB window opens. Can pledge or sell RE without violating secured debt covenants. DCs available for SLB or standalone sale. $360M cash needed for payoff (from operations or revolver).
May 15, 2031
4.625% notes mature		 $500M unsecured gone. Rating step-up covenant risk eliminated (already +175bps from downgrades). Restricted payment baskets expand further. ~$45M/yr additional interest savings. Full capital allocation flexibility. Remaining $640M is long-dated (2033-2045), low-coupon, no step-up provisions. Company can increase dividends, execute buybacks, or distribute SLB proceeds as special dividend.
POST-2031 Remaining debt: 6.0% 2033 ($112M), 6.88% 2037 ($101M), 5.55% 2045 ($427M). No secured debt. No step-up covenants. Manageable interest burden (~$40M/yr). Residual debt fully serviceable from ongoing operations even in bear case. Clean balance sheet enables REIT conversion, spin-off, or full SLB program.
The sequence matters: 4.25% notes (✓ repaid FY2025) → 10% secured notes (Jun 2030, releases collateral) → 4.625% notes (May 2031, removes step-up risk). After May 2031, Kohl's has $640M of quiet long-dated debt and full freedom to monetize $5B+ of unencumbered RE. Special dividend potential: ~$42.37/share vs $13.04 stock.
